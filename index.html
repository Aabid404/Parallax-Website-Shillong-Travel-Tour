Thangkharang Park

Famous for panoramic views of the plains of Bangladesh.

Thangkharang Park, established to showcase Meghalaya's biodiversity and landscape, became a popular attraction by the late 20th century, allowing visitors to experience breathtaking views of the Bangladesh plains and the cascading Kynrem Falls. Maintained by the State Forest Department, the park is a serene spot featuring a Khoh Ramrah rock, viewpoints, and a small bridge, making it a significant site for both nature lovers and families, and a key part of Cherrapunji's tourism.

Sohra | Kshaid Khmat Kharai (Double Drive) Falls

Located in Sohra (Cherrapunji), Kshaid Khmat Kharai, also known as the Double Drive Falls, is a unique twin cascade where two parallel streams plunge side by side, creating a stunning natural view. It’s a popular scenic stop for travelers exploring the waterfalls of Meghalaya.

Mawsmai Cave

Explore the limestone formations of this stunning cave.

Mawsmai Cave is a natural limestone wonder near Cherrapunji, Meghalaya, known for its stalactite and stalagmite formations, easy access, and spiritual significance for the indigenous Khasi tribe. While the cave's geological origins are ancient and tied to water and wind erosion, it was only opened to tourists in the 1970s, becoming a major attraction that balances accessibility with the preservation of its fragile ecosystem.

Nohkalikai Falls

Witness India’s tallest plunge waterfall.

The Nohkalikai Falls story is a tragic folk tale about a woman named Likai who was tricked by her evil step-father into eating her own daughter, who he had murdered. Heartbroken and filled with unbearable grief after realizing the horrifying truth, Likai jumped from the top of the waterfall, and it was named Nohkalikai, meaning "the leap of Likai".
